PUP Physics Society Initiates “LINKnayan”

By Alyssandra Molina
Tue, October 13, 2020

To keep the bond among Physics students of Polytechnic University of the Philippines, the PUP Physics Society (PhySoc) launched LINKnayan: Phy-sically Bonded despite the Distance, an online event held via Zoom Cloud Meetings last June 13, 2020.

Spearheaded by PhySoc President Marc Robert Casero and alumni Raymond Ordinario, the event centred on career path discussions of the said program.

Participated by students, faculty members and former members of the organization, the event highlighted online chats, talks, and Q&A discussions with three alumni who shared expertise from their respective fields.

Mark Anthony Burgonio and Joseph Michael Nepomuceno who both ventured the field of medical physics talked about the roles of medical physicist while Nepomuceno discussed the challenges of the field in a hospital setting especially during this pandemic.

Meanwhile, Dr. Jeffrey De Vero shared his journey as an undergraduate student, and personal experiences as a Senior Research scientist at Ceres Power Company in United Kingdom.

Furthermore, two physics students shared their respective innovative projects as a way to alleviate the impact of the lockdown. Luther Villacruz, incoming third year student, introduced Project SPARTA while Faye Ugot, incoming second year student, imparted her Poemdemic project.

To conclude the event, Cyrus Ayala, Physics teacher and adviser of the PUP Physoc, discussed the possible system and scenarios expected to happen during the A.Y. 2020-2021 where the mode of learning is shifted to online and correspondence approach.

The PUP Physics Society plans to initiate online events similar to LINKnayan to ease academic responsibilities in the months to come.
